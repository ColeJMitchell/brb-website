Groups: Easton Moms
Name: Wild N'Free Lehigh

From the Wild + Free website: Ralph Waldo Emerson wrote, “All good things are wild and free.” This quote is the inspiration for the Wild & Free community because it depicts an emerging group of mothers and homeschoolers who want their children to not only receive a quality education, but also to experience the adventure, freedom, and wonder of childhood.

Rules: The purpose of this group is not to be a "general" homeschool group, but rather to be a small community of friends who gather and communicate on a regular basis. Please make it a priority to join gatherings and events when you can. There will never be "mandatory attendance" but this is not a group to just keep your socializing options open. Dialogue, chat, ask questions, share fun events in the area! All of these are so welcome and necessary to creating friendships.

Groups: Lehigh Valley Homeschooling Moms
Name: Little Village, Bethlehem

We provide daytime support for any primary carer who is parenting a child or children between the ages of 0 and 6 years old. This can include homemaking or part-time working moms or dads, grandparents, working parents with non-traditional schedules, or any parent who simply prefers a different moniker. Nannies are also welcome to join!

Rules: Members are expected and encouraged to add their own play dates and meetups to the calendar. In order to foster a sense of community and accountability to each other, we ask that all members commit to attending at least one event per month. Inactive members will routinely be removed, but are more than welcome to rejoin Little Village if availability/interest changes in the future.

Groups: New Mothers, Children Under 6, Nannies




                                   

About Us:


We are committed to bringing free family-child learning support to economically disadvantaged families who have little or no access to early learning opportunities. Our approach is learner- centered, teacher-guided and community supported. Working in partnership with families, we can build confidence in the family’s role as first teacher by providing support and education on the fundamentals of how children learn and child development. Our developmentally appropriate approach for children will enlist creativity, conversation, and play. Materials for continued home exploration and learning will be provided. We will support the need for early intervention and other social services.
